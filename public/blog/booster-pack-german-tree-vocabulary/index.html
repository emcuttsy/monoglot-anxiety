Booster Pack: German tree vocabulary

An Anki deck of 45 free German flashcards all about trees. Images, example sentences, color-coding, and cloze deletions for every word—and now audio, too.

March 5, 2021

Elise Cutts

5-Minute Read

Twitter

Facebook

Reddit

Pinterest

Email

When we learn a new language, we focus on learning the words we actually need to communicate. But eventually, you might want to be able to get across points like “pine tree” without having to say things like “der Baum, der Nadeln statt Blätter hat.”

This week’s booster pack is a deck full of eventually words involving trees—both the German names for specific types of trees and the German words for parts of trees like roots and branches.

Because, sure, you can get by with just “Baum”. But where’s the fun in that?

Note: Anki decks now have German audio for words and example sentences

Starting with this booster pack, all Anki decks will have German audio going forward. I’m currently working to create audio for the last two booster packs as well, and will update my flashcard template to incorperate space for audio files soon.

If you use Duolingo, you’ll recognize the voice. I use Amazon Polly for text-to-speech, which is the service that powers Duolingo.

You can download the deck for free or pay-what-you-want. The deck includes images and example sentences for every word, plays German audio for all words and example sentences, and is based on a reusable flashcard template that automatically color-codes nouns by gender, highlights irregular verbs, and creates cloze deletions from example sentences without an extra click.


Get the deck


The vocabulary list is provided below if you’d prefer to make your own cards.

A funny song to help you learn German names for trees: “Das Sägewerk Bad Segeberg” by the Wise Guys

The idea for this week’s deck came from a goofy German song about a sawmill. “Das Sägewerk Bad Segeberg” by the Wise Guys packs about as many different trees into the lyrics as possibe for a 2:45 minute-long song. After my tutor shared the song with me a few weeks ago, I just had to make a deck about trees.

I’ve made sure to include every type of tree mentioned in “Das Sägewerk Bad Segeberg” in the deck. So after you’ve studied up a bit, give the song a listen—you should recognize a lot of new words.

Quick note: there isn’t actually a sawmill in the real German town of Bad Segeberg. But there is a forestry school, and they made a pretty hilarious music video of their own based on the original.
